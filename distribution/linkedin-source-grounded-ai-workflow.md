## Post

Most AI study workflows start in the wrong place.

They ask for a summary first.

That produces something that feels useful, but it often hides the most important question: which part of the source supports this claim?

I have been testing a more source-grounded order:

1. Use NotebookLM to build a source map.
2. Mark claims that are not clearly supported.
3. Use ChatGPT only after that to create retrieval-practice questions.
4. Keep a failure log instead of polishing weak claims into confident prose.

The point is not to make prettier notes.

The point is to preserve the source trail long enough to study from the material without confusing explanation with evidence.

I put the workflow, prompt, screenshots, outputs, scoring sheet, and unsupported-claims log here:

https://practical-ai-workflows.pages.dev/notebooklm-chatgpt-pdf-study-evidence/

Would be interested in how other people are handling source-grounded AI workflows for PDFs, policies, research papers, and lecture notes.
