# Practical AI Workflows — Content Factory Plan

## Reset

The site is not a single NotebookLM/ChatGPT study experiment. That first URL is only the launch wedge. The business goal is a continuous, agent-assisted publishing and distribution factory:

1. produce many useful English pages across several traffic categories,
2. keep quality high enough for Google indexing and AdSense review,
3. mix traffic-first posts with money-first posts,
4. ship downloadable assets/templates/calculators wherever possible,
5. distribute each post with community-native versions,
6. measure impressions, clicks, downloads, referrals, and eventual revenue.

## Agent advantage over a human writer

A human advantage is taste, judgment, and lived experience. The agent advantage is operating leverage:

- never gets tired of repetitive SERP/source/brief/draft/QA passes,
- can maintain hundreds of keyword/article states without forgetting,
- can generate multiple angles and formats per topic,
- can run parallel research/review workers,
- can enforce templates, disclosures, noindex/index gates, internal links, sitemap, and GA4/GSC checks,
- can repurpose one article into Reddit comments, X/Threads, LinkedIn, short scripts, and email snippets,
- can keep updating losers/winners based on Search Console instead of vibes.

This only works if the factory ships public useful assets, not internal meta-docs.

## Publishing mix

For every 10 posts:

- 3 traffic-first posts: broad AI/how-to/study/productivity queries
- 3 money-first posts: SaaS comparison, tool stack, buying decision
- 2 template posts: downloadable checklist/prompt/calculator
- 1 trend-capture post: fast-moving tool/workflow update
- 1 internal-link hub/listicle: clusters existing posts

## Per-post artifact package

Each published unit should have:

1. article markdown,
2. one useful asset: template/checklist/prompt/calculator/table,
3. 3 title variants,
4. meta description,
5. FAQ block,
6. internal links to 2-5 related pages,
7. source links / evidence note,
8. Reddit-safe discussion version,
9. X/Threads 5-post thread,
10. LinkedIn version,
11. short-video script,
12. GA4/GSC metrics row.

## Minimum anti-slop gate

Publish fast, but never publish pages that are:

- just generic AI advice,
- unsupported claims or fake benchmarks,
- copied competitor structure with synonyms,
- no original template/checklist/table,
- no clear user job-to-be-done,
- no internal links,
- no measurement row.

## 30-day target

- Ship 30 useful public URLs.
- At least 10 with downloadable assets.
- At least 10 money/revenue-intent pages.
- Submit sitemap regularly.
- One distribution attempt per public URL.
- Weekly: kill/update/expand based on GSC and referral data.
