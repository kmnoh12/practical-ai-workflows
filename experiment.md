# Experiment

## Hypothesis

People who study from PDFs want a practical NotebookLM + ChatGPT workflow, not just a tool comparison. A public article plus a free workflow/template should produce measurable search, referral, download, signup, or direct feedback signals within 30 days.

## Asset

One publishing unit:

1. public-ready article: `notebooklm-vs-chatgpt-for-studying-pdfs`;
2. free PDF Study Workflow Template;
3. one Reddit/community version;
4. one X/Threads thread;
5. one LinkedIn post;
6. one short-video script;
7. simple measurement note.

## Primary metric

Any real external behavior within 30 days:

- GSC impressions;
- organic/referral clicks;
- template downloads;
- email signups;
- direct user feedback;
- tool/template usage signal.

## Success criteria

Continue if at least one happens within 30 days:

- 100+ GSC impressions;
- 5+ organic/referral clicks;
- 10+ template downloads;
- 3+ email signups;
- 3+ direct feedback responses;
- one clear request for a tool/template/product extension.

## Failure criteria

Pivot or kill if after 30 days:

- impressions/clicks/downloads/signups are near zero;
- feedback says the workflow is unclear or not useful;
- traffic exists but no one wants the template;
- the project produces more internal docs than public-facing assets.

## Current decision

Split accepted: business experiment and Hermes/Codex operations lab are separate.

- Business experiment lives in this root README, this `experiment.md`, `metrics.md`, and the public-facing article/template assets.
- Hermes/Codex evidence and QA history is frozen under `automation-lab-archive/`.

## Do not do for the next 30 days

- no new gate systems;
- no new phase plans;
- no new readiness models;
- no broad content pipeline docs;
- no extra articles until the first publishing unit ships and gets a distribution attempt.
